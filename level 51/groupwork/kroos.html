Toni Kroos

Early Life and Background Full Name: Toni Kroos
Date of Birth: January 4, 1990
Place of Birth: Greifswald, Germany
Height: 1.82 m (6 feet)
Position: Central Midfielder
Toni Kroos was born in the small city of Greifswald, in the northeastern part of Germany, where he developed his passion for football at a young age. His family was deeply involved in sports, with his father, Roland Kroos, a former footballer and coach, and his younger brother, Felix Kroos, also a professional footballer. Kroos began playing football at the Greifswalder SV before joining Hansa Rostock’s youth academy, where his talent was quickly recognized.
Early Career and Move to Bayern Munich
Youth Career: Kroos joined the youth academy of Hansa Rostock at the age of 10, and his exceptional talent soon saw him make the move to Bayern Munich's youth system in 2006, at the age of 16. Bayern Munich had been scouting him for years, and they saw immense potential in him, offering him a chance to continue his development at one of Europe’s biggest clubs.
Bayern Munich (2007-2009): Kroos made his professional debut for Bayern Munich in the 2007-2008 season at just 18 years old. However, given his age and the stiff competition for a first-team spot, he was loaned out to Bayer Leverkusen for the 2009-2010 season to gain valuable experience in the Bundesliga. During this loan spell, Kroos developed into one of the league’s most talented midfielders, showcasing his passing range, vision, and ability to control the tempo of a match.
Breakthrough at Bayern Munich
Return to Bayern (2010-2014): After a successful loan spell, Kroos returned to Bayern Munich for the 2010-2011 season, where he quickly became a mainstay in the midfield. Under then-manager Louis van Gaal, Kroos flourished and became known for his technical mastery, ability to dictate play, and precise passing. His long-range passes, free-kicks, and vision helped him make an immediate impact.
Kroos was a key part of Bayern’s successes during this period, including winning the Bundesliga and DFB-Pokal titles in 2013. He played a pivotal role in Bayern Munich’s historic 2013 UEFA Champions League victory, with his performances in the midfield becoming a defining feature of the team.
Real Madrid Move and Success (2014-Present)
Transfer to Real Madrid (2014): In 2014, after winning numerous domestic trophies with Bayern Munich, Kroos made a high-profile move to Real Madrid for a transfer fee of around €25 million—a relatively modest fee for a player of his quality. His arrival at Madrid coincided with a period of change and rebuilding under coach Carlo Ancelotti.
Immediate Impact at Real Madrid: In his first season at Madrid, Kroos quickly established himself as one of the best midfielders in the world. His deep-lying playmaker role, often sitting just in front of the defense, allowed him to dictate the rhythm of the game with exceptional passing and movement. He was integral to Real Madrid’s 2014 UEFA Champions League victory, helping the club secure its La Décima (tenth European Cup/Champions League title) under Ancelotti.
Success Under Various Managers (2014-Present): Over the years, Kroos has continued to be a central figure at Real Madrid, contributing to multiple Champions League titles, La Liga championships, and Copa del Rey victories. He was a key part of Madrid’s dominance in European football during the 2010s, with the club winning four Champions League titles in five years (2014, 2016, 2017, 2018).
Key Attributes:
Passing & Vision: Kroos is widely regarded as one of the best passers in the world. His ability to control the tempo of a match with his passing range—whether short, long, or through balls—has set him apart as a central midfielder. He’s known for making decisive passes that create goal-scoring opportunities.
Ball Retention & Composure: Kroos is a master at retaining possession and evading pressure. His ability to remain composed under intense pressure allows him to make accurate passes even in difficult situations.
Free-Kicks & Set-Pieces: Known for his quality in delivering precise free-kicks and corner kicks, Kroos can score goals from set-pieces and provide key assists in dead-ball situations.
International Career
Germany National Team (2010-Present): Kroos made his international debut for Germany in 2010 and has since become one of the pillars of the national team. He was an integral part of Germany’s 2014 FIFA World Cup winning team. Kroos played a pivotal role in the tournament, scoring two goals against Brazil in the 2014 World Cup semifinals, which included one of the most iconic goals in World Cup history—a stunning long-range shot that flew into the net from outside the penalty box.
UEFA Euro 2016 & 2020: Although Germany did not win the 2016 UEFA European Championship, Kroos was a key player in the tournament. He continued to show his importance in future competitions, including the UEFA Euro 2020 and the 2022 FIFA World Cup qualifiers, where he remained an important part of the German national team.
International Honors:
FIFA World Cup (2014)
UEFA European Championship (2012 – Runner-up)
FIFA Confederations Cup (2017)
Kroos announced that he would step back from international football after the 2020 UEFA European Championship, citing a desire to focus more on his club career with Real Madrid. He has since been a key figure in the evolution of Germany's midfield.
Playing Style
Toni Kroos is often referred to as one of the most complete central midfielders in modern football. His deep-lying playmaker role allows him to control the tempo of matches and serve as the link between defense and attack. Vision and Passing: Kroos is an exceptional passer, and his vision allows him to spot opportunities that most players miss. He’s known for his ability to launch counterattacks with long passes and make incisive through balls to forwards. His passing accuracy is exceptional, both in short exchanges and long-range balls.
Composure: One of his most notable qualities is his calmness and ability to maintain control of the ball under pressure. Kroos has an extraordinary sense of balance and positioning, which allows him to make quick decisions in tight spaces.
Tactical Awareness: Kroos understands the game at an advanced level. He knows when to press and when to drop deeper to collect the ball. His intelligence allows him to break down opposition attacks and seamlessly transition the play forward.

Personal Life
Toni Kroos is known for his down-to-earth and private nature, despite his fame. He is married to Jessica Farber, with whom he has children. He is also an avid sports fan and enjoys golf in his free time. Kroos has built a reputation for being highly professional, always focused on his craft, and maintaining a sense of humility despite his immense success.
Legacy and Future
As of 2025, Toni Kroos remains one of the most respected and influential midfielders in world football. His ability to dictate play, combined with his impressive trophy collection, makes him one of the best midfielders of his generation. Despite his age (35 in 2025), Kroos still plays at a high level for Real Madrid, with many considering him one of the best players of the 2010s era.
Kroos’s legacy is one of intelligence, precision, and consistency. As a player who has won everything there is to win at both the club and international level, he is likely to be remembered as one of the best midfielders in football history.
